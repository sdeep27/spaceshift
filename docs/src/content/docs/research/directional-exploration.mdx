---
title: Directional Exploration
description: Navigate prompt space with subprompt, superprompt, sideprompt, and prompt_tree.
---

Most prompt decomposition only goes downward. spaceshift moves in three directions.

```python
from spaceshift import subprompt, superprompt, sideprompt, prompt_tree

# Down — decompose into focused subtopics
subs = subprompt("What are the effects of AI on labor markets?", n=[5])

# Up — discover the bigger question this is a piece of
supers = superprompt("What are the effects of AI on labor markets?", n=[3])

# Sideways — explore sibling questions at the same abstraction level
sides = sideprompt("What are the effects of AI on labor markets?", n=[4])

# All three at once, with tree visualization
tree = prompt_tree(
    "What are the effects of AI on labor markets?",
    sub_n=[5], super_n=[3], side_n=[4],
    viz=True,
)
```

All directional functions default to `search="auto"` — web search is enabled automatically when the model supports it.

## Outline

- `subprompt(prompt, n=[])` — decompose downward into subtopics
- `superprompt(prompt, n=[])` — generalize upward to broader framings
- `sideprompt(prompt, n=[])` — lateral exploration of sibling questions
- `prompt_tree()` — all three directions combined
- Multi-level decomposition with nested `n` values (e.g., `n=[5, 3]`)
- `search="auto"` — auto-detects model web search support
- Tree visualization with graphviz
- Why superprompt and sideprompt are novel contributions
- Practical research workflows combining all three
